Features

Modeled, not faked

A custom Metal engine computes the real mechanics per pixel — spectral exposure, Hurter–Driffield response, dye absorption, grain, and halation. No tone-curve shortcut.

Full control

Every look is a profile you build, not a preset you scroll. Tune sensitizer wavelength, dye, contrast, grain, and halation — layer by layer.

Built for the iPhone camera

Designed around the device's native capture pipeline and made to push it — shoot from the Camera Control button and straight from the lock screen, with the full reach of the camera you already carry.

Re-develop anytime

Every shot remembers the profile it was made with, so you can re-develop or re-tune it later — non-destructively, on the original capture.

Share as a code

Hand a profile you built to a friend as a single scannable code — no export, no cloud, no account.

Everything on device

No account, no servers, no tracking. Your photos, videos, and profiles never leave your phone.

Support

Email support

How do I save photos to my library?

Turn on Save to Photos in settings. Oqra keeps your shots in its own private folder by default and only writes to your system Photos library when this is enabled.

Does Oqra collect or upload my data?

No. There's no account and no server. Your photos, videos, and recipes stay on your device. See the Privacy Policy for the full breakdown.

How do I shoot RAW?

Enable the RAW Capture toggle. Oqra then captures in RAW / ProRAW and develops it through the film engine.

How do I share a film recipe?

Open a recipe and use Share to generate a scannable code. A friend with Oqra can scan it to load the same film stock — nothing is uploaded. Received one without the app? Open it here.

Which iPhones does Oqra support?

Oqra runs on recent iPhones; the Camera Control button requires an iPhone 16.

I found a bug or have a feature request.

Please email fantasyqkm@gmail.com with your device model and iOS version, and a screenshot if you can.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 1, 2026

Oqra is a film-emulation photo and video app. This policy explains what information the app collects and how it is used.

What we collect

Nothing is sent off your device. Oqra does not require an account, does not transmit your photos, videos, or film recipes to any server, and does not include third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

The app requests the following system permissions, used only for the features you choose to enable:

  • Camera — to capture photos and video inside Oqra, including via the iPhone Camera Control button and the lock-screen Locked Camera Capture extension.
  • Microphone — to record audio when capturing video. Not used during photo capture.
  • Photo Library — only if you turn on "Save to Photos." When enabled, the developed photo or video is saved to your system Photos library. Oqra does not read your existing photos or videos unless you import one through the in-app picker.
  • Location, Contacts — not used.

What is stored on your device

  • Photos and videos you capture or import, and the developed media produced by the engine, in Oqra's app-private folder.
  • Film recipes you create or favorite, and the recipe snapshots locked to each roll.
  • App preferences (such as the RAW Capture toggle and the Save to Photos toggle).

This data stays on your device. You can delete all of it at any time by removing the app.

TestFlight builds

If you are using Oqra through TestFlight, Apple collects diagnostic and usage information per Apple's own TestFlight beta tester data practices. Oqra itself does not receive this information.

Children

Oqra is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change. Material changes will be noted in the app's release notes.
