Capture, then develop

A camera on one side, a darkroom on the other.

Load a film, point, and shoot. Your iPhone captures the frame; Oqra develops it live in the viewfinder the instant you press the shutter — a real film engine modeled on spectral exposure, density curves, dye, grain, and halation. What you see is what you shoot — no editing step, no filter laid on top.

An engine, not a filter

A physics-based film simulation, not a lookup table: a native RAW decoder feeding a custom Metal engine that runs real photographic mechanics per pixel. The look is computed from the light.

Build your own film, then share it

Every look is a film recipe you can open and author — emulsion layers, dye, grain, response. Build your own, then share it as a QR code or link. Someone scans it and shoots your film.
